Islamic Studies

Deepen your understanding of Islamic principles, history, and teachings through comprehensive and respectful instruction

Course Overview

Our Islamic Studies course provides a comprehensive, respectful, and scholarly approach to understanding Islam. The curriculum covers foundational beliefs, practices, history, and contemporary applications of Islamic teachings, making it suitable for Muslims seeking to deepen their knowledge and non-Muslims interested in learning about Islam.

We approach Islamic Studies with reverence, academic rigor, and cultural sensitivity, ensuring that students gain a well-rounded understanding while respecting the sacred nature of the subject matter.

Who This Course Is For

  • Muslims seeking to deepen their understanding of their faith
  • Children and teenagers learning about Islam
  • New Muslims or those considering conversion
  • Non-Muslims interested in learning about Islam
  • Parents wanting to teach their children about Islam
  • Students preparing for Islamic studies exams or programs

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand the fundamental beliefs and principles of Islam
  • Learn about the Five Pillars of Islam and their significance
  • Study the life of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him)
  • Understand Islamic history and civilization
  • Explore Islamic ethics, values, and jurisprudence
  • Gain knowledge of Islamic practices and rituals
  • Appreciate the diversity within the Muslim community
  • Apply Islamic principles to contemporary life

Curriculum Outline

Foundations of Islam

  • Shahada (Declaration of Faith)
  • Core beliefs (Tawheed, Prophethood, Afterlife)
  • Introduction to the Qur'an and Hadith

The Five Pillars

  • Shahada (Faith)
  • Salah (Prayer)
  • Zakat (Charity)
  • Sawm (Fasting)
  • Hajj (Pilgrimage)

Prophetic History

  • Life of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him)
  • Early Islamic history
  • Companions of the Prophet

Islamic Law & Ethics

  • Introduction to Shariah and Fiqh
  • Islamic ethics and morality
  • Contemporary applications

Islamic Civilization

  • Contributions to science, art, and culture
  • Islamic history and empires
  • Muslim communities around the world

Contemporary Issues

  • Islam in the modern world
  • Addressing misconceptions
  • Living as a Muslim today

Teaching Approach

Our Islamic Studies course is taught with respect, authenticity, and academic rigor:

  • Respectful Instruction: Teaching with reverence for sacred texts and traditions
  • Authentic Sources: Using primary sources and scholarly interpretations
  • Age-Appropriate: Content adapted for different age groups
  • Inclusive Environment: Welcoming students from all backgrounds
  • Discussion-Based: Encouraging questions and thoughtful dialogue
  • Practical Application: Connecting teachings to daily life

Session Format

  • Duration: Sessions typically last 60-90 minutes
  • Frequency: Flexible scheduling (1-2 sessions per week)
  • Format: Live online sessions via video conferencing
  • Class Size: Small groups for discussion and Q&A
  • Materials: Authentic texts, scholarly resources, and age-appropriate materials
  • Assignments: Reflection papers, research projects, and discussions

Course-Specific FAQ

Do I need to be Muslim to take this course?

No, this course is open to everyone. We welcome Muslims seeking to deepen their knowledge and non-Muslims interested in learning about Islam in a respectful, academic setting.

Is this course appropriate for children?

Yes! We offer age-appropriate Islamic Studies courses for children, with content and teaching methods suited to their developmental level.

What school of thought does this course follow?

Our course presents mainstream Islamic teachings while respecting the diversity of scholarly opinions. We focus on common ground and core principles shared across different schools of thought.
